Personal Pursuits

Know Me Better

I’ve always believed that the best digital strategies are informed by a life lived with intention outside the boardroom. These are the personal rituals and curiosities that keep my perspective sharp and my mindset flexible.

Traveling

There is a specific kind of clarity that only comes when you are miles away from a paved road and hours into a steep climb.

To me, travel is a deliberate act of stripping away the familiar to see what’s left. I find as much joy in the "ritual of research"—the meticulous mapping and the spreadsheets—as I do in the journey itself. I am entirely experience-driven, choosing the rugged authenticity of a remote trekking trail or a low-key homestay over luxury. I believe the most meaningful discoveries happen when you respect the local rhythm, embrace the silence of a long hike, and remain flexible enough to let the experience change you.

Photography

I’ve always felt that photography isn’t about what you see, but how you choose to look at it.

It is my favorite way to reset my cognitive "refresh rate" by finding a narrative in the subtle details others might skip. Whether I am behind the lens or in the technical phase of post-processing, I am focused on capturing the atmosphere of a moment rather than just a static image. It is a constant exercise in patience—refining raw data into a story that holds the weight and emotion of the original experience.

Captured Perspectives / @illusion_shots

Cooking

I treat the kitchen as a creative laboratory where there are no "undo" buttons—only iterations.

I love the challenge of researching a complex, unfamiliar dish and attempting to recreate it from scratch, blending traditional methods with my own experiments. It is a process of high-level planning met with the courage to be messy. Reaching that final result after hours of preparation is a constant reminder that the best innovations often come from staying curious, embracing trial and error, and trusting the process.
